Solving FPB puzzle by hand is super fun!

Here are some common patterns and tips:

  • Guesses Response
    1234 F
    7254 PP

    In the first row, if 2 is Fermi, then 2 would also be Fermi in the second row. But the second row does not have a Fermi, so 2 cannot be Fermi in either row. The first row only has one Fermi, so 2 must be a Bagel.

    By the same reason, 4 must be a Bagel.

    From the second row, since both 2 and 4 are Bagels, then 7 and 5 must both be Picos. That tells us two things:

    1. Both 7 and 5 are in the secret number, so only two other numbers are unknown.
    2. 7 is not the first digit. 5 is not the third digit.

    XX36 P

    7XXX F

    From the second and third rows, we know 7 must be a Bagel.

    From the second row, since 7 is a Bagel, there must be another Bagel in 9, 3 and 6.

    Suppose 3 and 6 are both Picos, then they are also Picos in the first row. But first row only has one Pico. So 3 and 6 cannot both be Picos. So 9 must be a Pico, and the other Pico is in 3 and 6.

    From the first row, since there is one Pico in 3 and 6, then the two X must be Bagel.
